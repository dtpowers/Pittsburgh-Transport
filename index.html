Navigating PGH

The T

What it's best for: It is best for commuting between three main areas: Downtown, Station Square and the North Shore. The T is especially convenient for sporting events such as a Pirates or Steelers game.

What it is: The T is a newer rail system designed to alleviate some of the crowds on port authority buses. Two trains run Monday through Friday and for games and special events.

What it costs: Free

Instructions on how to ride:
  1. Plan your trip by finding the stations you will board and depart at ahead of time http://www.portauthority.org/paac/apps/maps/TLines.pdf
  2. Arrive at your boarding station and wait for the train away from the rail lines in one of the designated boarding areas. Note: You should expect to wait no more than 10-15 minutes between trains.
  3. When the train arrives, allow passengers to first exit and then board the train.
  4. The train conductor and electronic board will announce the upcoming stops. Listen for your destination and exit when the train comes to a complete stop.

CMU Shuttle

What it's best for: Specific destination trips to Shadyside, Oakland and Bakery Square.

What it is: A shuttle service run by the university to aide students in commuting to and from popular destinations in the surrounding campus area.

What it costs: Free with a CMU ID (covered in your Transportation Fee)

Instructions on how to ride:
  1. Plan your trip by finding the stop you will board and get off the CMU Shuttle https://www.cmu.edu/police/shuttleandescort/b-route.html (stops and times available)
  2. Arrive at your stop at least 5 minutes before the scheduled time as the shuttles cannot arrive to the exact listed minute.
  3. When the shuttle arrives, first allow passengers to exit and then board by showing the driver your CMU ID.
  4. Inform the driver where you plan to get off and enjoy your ride.

Uber

What it’s best for: Short rides, getting somewhere quickly, travelling with a small group (3-5) of friends, getting a ride late at night.

What it is: Uber is a popular application that crowdsources local drivers to pick you up and take you to a destination.

What it costs: Uber costs are all about supply and demand. They have a system called Surge Pricing which applies a multiplier to standard fees at their busiest times. You will always see the Surge multiplier and must agree to it before taking an Uber. There is usually a $5 - $10 minimum on Uber rides and you can split the fare with all riders if they have the app.

Instructions on how to ride:
  1. Download Uber's App from the Apple App Store or Google Play Store.
  2. If you have an account, skip to step 3, otherwise input your name, email, phone number and credit card information that will be charged for rides.
  3. Once you are logged in, you can set a pickup location by inputting your current address or accepting the default address Uber finds for you.
  4. Also select an uberX for a max of 4 riders or an uberXL for a max of 6 riders.
  5. Press “Set Pickup Location” to confirm your options for this ride.
  6. The app will display the approximate pickup time and show nearby cars. If everything is correct press "REQUEST uberX".
  7. The app will show the car approaching and you will usually get a call from the driver when they arrive.
  8. All payments are handled through the app so the remaining step is to input your destination or simply tell your driver where to take you and thank them once you've arrived.

Zipcar

What it's best for: Extended travel, travel with groups, transportation of items, late night/early morning travel.
Ikea - When furnishing a dorm the bus isn’t conducive to hauling things. Rental of a Zip Car allows temporary access to a van or a truck for moving needs.
Day Trips - Looking to go somewhere outside Pittsburgh just for the day? Zip Car allows you to rent a car for a day trip cheaper than other car rental, and if you are under 21 it’s your only rental option.

What it is: Zip Car is a short term car rental service that charges for hourly use. The abundance of car pickup locations in the Pittsburgh area means there is almost always one walking distance away. Unlike most car rental services anyone 18 and up can rent car via their university program.

What it costs: 7$ monthly + 7-10$ per hour

Instructions on how to ride:
  1. Register for an account + download the mobile application
  2. Select the time period to reserve a car
  3. Select a model of car to reserve
  4. Travel to the pickup location of your reserved car
  5. Unlock and start the car from the mobile application OR with your zipcar card
  6. Return Car before end of reservation

The Bus

What it’s best for: Short trips, day time travel, travel when you aren’t in a hurry,

Water Front - Grabbing any 61 from CMU OUTBOUND can get you to the Water Front in about 30 min
Squirrel Hill - Grabbing a 61 A or B OUTBOUND from CMU can get you to Squirrel Hill in 10 min
Downtown - Taking any 61 INBOUND can get you downtown in 22 min.
What it is: Port Authority buses are the primary means of travel for most CMU students as well as a large part of the pittsburgh population. This service is free to all CMU students and can get you most places you need to go. Be wary however, busses can be slow, inconsistent, and unreliable past midnight. Plan accordingly.

What it costs: Free with student ID, $2.50 without

Instructions on how to ride:
  1. Determine appropriate bus to take and proceed to nearest stop
  2. Wave to bus as it approaches to indicate you would like to board
  3. After allowing passengers to exit, enter and tap ID card against the orange RFID reader with a green light near the door
  4. As approaching stop, pull yellow cord to indicate you would like to exit
  5. Exit bus

Bike Rental

What it's best for: Travel in good weather, short trips, leisurely travel
Squirrel HIll - This service is best for relatively close locations, places like squirrel hill are ideal if you don’t want to walk or take the bus.

What it is: Healthy Ride is a bike rental system where users can pick up a bike from any station and then leave it at any other station.

What it costs: $2 per ½ hour, or $12 a month for unlimited 30 min rides

Instructions on how to ride:
  1. Register for an account + download the mobile app from healthyridepgh.com
  2. Go to the nearest Healthy Ride station and either Scan the QR code on the back of a bike OR Enter your phone number on the bike num pad
  3. Ride bike to destination, return bike before reservation expires
  4. Repeat same process as renting bike to end reservation
