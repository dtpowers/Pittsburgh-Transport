Navigating PGH

The T

What it's best for: It is best for commuting between three main areas: Downtown, Station Square and the North Shore. The T is especially convenient for sporting events such as a Pirates or Steelers game.

What it is: The T is a newer rail system designed to alleviate some of the crowds on port authority buses. Two trains run Monday through Friday and for games and special events.

What it costs: Free

Instructions on how to ride:
  1. Plan your trip by finding the stations you will board and depart at ahead of time http://www.portauthority.org/paac/apps/maps/TLines.pdf
  2. Arrive at your boarding station and wait for the train away from the rail lines in one of the designated boarding areas. Note: You should expect to wait no more than 10-15 minutes between trains.
  3. When the train arrives, allow passengers to first exit and then board the train.
  4. The train conductor and electronic board will announce the upcoming stops. Listen for your destination and exit when the train comes to a complete stop.

CMU Shuttle

What it's best for: Specific destination trips to Shadyside, Oakland and Bakery Square.

What it is: A shuttle service run by the university to aide students in commuting to and from popular destinations in the surrounding campus area.

What it costs: Free with a CMU ID (covered in your Transportation Fee)

Instructions on how to ride:
  1. Plan your trip by finding the stop you will board and get off the CMU Shuttle https://www.cmu.edu/police/shuttleandescort/b-route.html (stops and times available)
  2. Arrive at your stop at least 5 minutes before the scheduled time as the shuttles cannot arrive to the exact listed minute.
  3. When the shuttle arrives, first allow passengers to exit and then board by showing the driver your CMU ID.
  4. Inform the driver where you plan to get off and enjoy your ride.
